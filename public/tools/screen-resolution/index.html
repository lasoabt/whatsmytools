Understanding Screen Resolution

Screen resolution refers to the number of pixels your display can show, typically expressed as width × height (e.g., 1920×1080). This tool shows both your physical screen resolution and your browser's viewport size, which may be different due to browser chrome, toolbars, and zoom levels.

Common Resolution Categories

  • HD (720p): 1280×720 pixels
  • Full HD (1080p): 1920×1080 pixels
  • 2K: 2560×1440 pixels
  • 4K (2160p): 3840×2160 pixels
  • 5K: 5120×2880 pixels
  • 8K: 7680×4320 pixels

Device Pixel Ratio

Modern high-density displays (like Retina displays) have a device pixel ratio greater than 1, meaning they pack more physical pixels into each logical pixel for sharper images.
